History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.).
Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning,
generating billions of dollars each year.

The Beginning (1950s)

The first computerized video game was called Bertie the Brain in 1950, which was played similarly to tic tac toe.
In 1952, the first complex video game was made, which was mathematician Alan Turing’s electronic chess game.
Finally in 1958, the first video game created for entertainment and utilizing game physics, the introduction
of the laws of physics into a simulation, was MIT’s space shooter game Spacewar!


Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

In 1967, a company called Sanders Associates, Inc. developed ‘The Brown Box,’ a prototype multiplayer video game console
that could be played on the television. The console was copyrighted by Magnavox, and sold as a multi program system called the Odyssey.
Incidentally, the smash hit arcade game Space Invaders was released in 1978. Eventually, the game would rack in 13 billion dollars in total revenue,
making it the highest grossing video game of all time. In 1981, the massively successful video game company Nintendo released Donkey Kong,
a game where many beloved characters such as Mario were introduced. Although the creation of the game Donkey Kong isn’t that significant,
the introduction to the video game empire that is the Mario franchise makes the release of the game historical.


North American Video Game Crash (1983)

Video game consoles that were available during the video game crash of 1983 included Odyssey, Intellivision, ColecoVision, Atari 5200, and Vectrex.


The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

While recovering from the video game crash, the 8-bit third generation of video games kicked off with Nintendo's Family Computer
(also known as Famicom) and Sega’s SG-1000. This was significant because it allowed for video games to remember a person’s progress.
In 1989, Nintendo made history by releasing the legendary Game Boy, which was a handheld 8-bit gaming console.
The Game Boy was so successful that it has sold 118.69 million copies. In 1994, the Entertainment Software Rating Board was created
after a Senate investigation of video games that contained crude and sexual content without proper warning.


Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

In 1995, Sony releases the PlayStation to the United States market, where it sold for $299. The first PlayStation has sold 120 million units.
This was the first console and hand-held game devices, and was massively successful. In 1999, a man named Billy Mitchell from Holyoke, Massachusetts
made video game history when he got a super score of 3,333,360 in the game Pac-Man, which is the highest score a player can get.
He was the first person to ever achieve this. Finally, in 2006, Ninterndo released the beloved video game system Wii,
which is a gaming system that lets gamers use the controller in revolutionary ways, such as swinging it like a tennis racket.
This was the first console to ever do this, and it made the Wii revolutionary. Nintendo’s Wii line continues to break records;
the original Wii was the fastest selling video game in history, which was later broken by Wii’s Nintendo Switch.


Today in Gaming (2010-Now)

Today we are in the fifth decade of video games. With continuous releasing of new technology, video games are being played on phones,
computers, arcades, and gaming consoles. In 2010, the OnLive gaming console was the first to exclusively feature cloud-based gaming,
which is when a game stays on a company server rather than on the gamer's computer or device. This has become standard in today's gaming.
After the release of the film Avatar, 3-D graphics began popping up in games everywhere. The first to do this was Nintendo with the 3-DS.
Virtual reality gaming has also become huge in the gaming market. Such companies that first released these games were Oculus with Rift,
HTC with Rive, and Sony with PlayStation VR. The most popular video games of the 2010s include Angry Birds, Minecraft, the Sims,
and last but not least, Fortnite. Fortnite, released in 2017, has become a pop culture phenomenon, inspiring dances, shirts, sweatshirts,
language patterns, and other sorts of merchandise. By the end of its first month, over 500,000 digital copies were sold.
